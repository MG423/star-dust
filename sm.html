A Timeline of Human Achievement Beyond Earth

1957 – 1959

The Dawn of Space
  1. 4 Oct 1957 USSR

    Sputnik 1

    • First artificial satellite
    • First human-made signals from space
  2. 3 Nov 1957 USSR

    Sputnik 2

    • First mammal in orbit — the dog Laika
  3. 31 Jan 1958 USA

    Explorer 1

    • Confirmed existence of the Van Allen radiation belt
  4. 17 Mar 1958 USA

    Vanguard 1

    • First use of solar power in space
    • Oldest artificial object still in orbit
  5. 4 Jan 1959 USSR

    Luna 1

    • First rocket to reach Earth escape velocity
    • First artificial object in heliocentric orbit
    • First detection of solar wind
  6. 13 Sep 1959 USSR

    Luna 2

    • First spacecraft to impact another celestial body (the Moon)
  7. 4 Oct 1959 USSR

    Luna 3

    • First photos of the far side of the Moon
    • First gravity assist

    Mariner 2

  6. 16 Jun 1963 USSR

    Vostok 6

  7. 18 Mar 1965 USSR

    Voskhod 2

    Mariner 4

    Gemini 6A / Gemini 7

    Gemini 8

    • First orbital docking between two spacecraft
  12. 3 Apr 1966 USSR

    Luna 10

    • First artificial satellite around another world (Moon)
  13. 30 Oct 1967 USSR

    Cosmos 186 / 188

    • First automated (crewless) docking
  14. 21 Dec 1968 USA

    Apollo 8

    Landmark
    • First humans beyond low Earth orbit

    Apollo 11

    Landmark

    • First space launch from another celestial body

    Mariner 9

1980 – 1989

Shuttles & Outer Planets
  1. 12 Apr 1981 USA

    STS-1 (Space Shuttle Columbia)

    Landmark
    • First reusable crewed orbital spacecraft
  2. 1 Mar 1982 USSR

    Venera 13

    • First Venus soil samples
    • First sound recording from another world
  3. 7 Feb 1984 USA

    STS-41-B

    • First untethered spacewalk — Bruce McCandless II
  4. 25 Jul 1984 USSR

    Salyut 7

    • First spacewalk by a woman — Svetlana Savitskaya

    ISEE-3 / ICE

    • First spacecraft to flyby a comet (21P/Giacobini-Zinner)

    Voyager 2 — Uranus Flyby

    • First spacecraft to flyby Uranus
  7. 19 Feb 1986 USSR

    Mir Space Station

    • First consistently inhabited long-term research space station
  8. 13 Mar 1986 ESA

    Giotto

    • First close-up observations of a comet — Halley's Comet (596 km)
  9. 25 Aug 1989 USA

    Voyager 2 — Neptune Flyby

    • First flyby of Neptune

  10. 18 Nov 1989 USA

    COBE

    • First orbital cosmic microwave background observatory

1990 – 1999

The Golden Age of Observatories
  1. 14 Feb 1990 USA

    Voyager 1 — Family Portrait

    • First photograph of the entire Solar System
  2. 24 Apr 1990 USA ESA

    Hubble Space Telescope

    Landmark
    • First telescope designed to be repaired in space
    • Transformed humanity's view of the universe
  3. 1992 Canada / Poland

    Wolszczan & Frail

    • First confirmed observation of an exoplanet
  4. 22 Mar 1995 Russia

    Mir — Valeri Polyakov

    • Record longest spaceflight — 437.7 consecutive days
  5. Jul 1995 Switzerland

    Mayor & Queloz

    • First confirmed exoplanet orbiting a main-sequence star (51 Pegasi b)
  6. 7 Dec 1995 USA

    Galileo

    • First orbit of Jupiter
    • First spacecraft to enter the atmosphere of a gas giant
  7. 4 Jul 1997 USA

    Mars Pathfinder / Sojourner

    • First operational rover on another planet (Mars)
  8. 20 Nov 1998 Multi-national

    International Space Station

    Landmark
    • First multinational space station
    • Largest artificial object ever built in space

2000 – 2009

Robots & Returning Samples
  1. 2 Nov 2000 Russia / USA

    NEAR Shoemaker

    Deep Impact

  5. 15 Jan 2006 USA

    Stardust

    • First sample return from a comet (81P/Wild)
  6. 8 Nov 2008 India

    Chandrayaan-1

    • First discovery of lunar water ice
  7. 6 Mar 2009 USA

    Kepler Mission

    • First space telescope dedicated to searching for Earth-like exoplanets
